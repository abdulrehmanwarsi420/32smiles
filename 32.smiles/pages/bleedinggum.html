Bleeding Gums


Buildup of dental plaque causes bleeding gums, a common symptom of gingivitis, periodontitis, and other forms of gum disease. Ignoring bleeding gums can lead to more severe issues like tooth loss. Regular dental check-ups and maintaining good oral hygiene can prevent and treat bleeding gums effectively.

Overview


What do bleeding gums mean?


Bleeding gums are the No. 1 symptom of gingivitis and other forms of gum disease. Gingivitis occurs when plaque builds up on teeth and leads to inflamed gums, which can bleed when brushing or flossing. While it is a common and often mild condition, untreated gingivitis can lead to more severe periodontal disease.

Other factors such as vitamin deficiencies, diabetes, and hormonal changes can also cause bleeding gums. Identifying the cause is crucial to determining the right treatment approach.

Possible Causes


Why are my gums bleeding?


There are several reasons why your gums might bleed:


  • Poor Oral Hygiene: Inadequate brushing and flossing can cause plaque buildup, leading to gum inflammation and bleeding.

  • Vitamin Deficiencies: A lack of vitamins C and K can result in gum bleeding.

  • Medical Conditions: Conditions like diabetes or blood disorders can cause bleeding gums.

  • Hormonal Changes: Pregnancy, menstruation, or menopause can make gums more susceptible to bleeding.

  • Medications: Blood-thinning medications and certain prescriptions can increase the risk of bleeding gums.

Understanding the root cause is essential for proper treatment and prevention.


Care and Treatment


How do healthcare providers treat bleeding gums?


Bleeding gums treatment depends on the underlying cause. Here are some common approaches:


  • Improved Oral Hygiene: Regular brushing, flossing, and professional cleanings can reduce plaque and inflammation.

  • Scaling and Root Planing: For severe gum disease, deep cleaning procedures like scaling and root planing may be necessary.

  • Medications: Antibiotics or medicated mouthwashes may be prescribed to treat infections and reduce inflammation.

  • Nutritional Supplements: If a vitamin deficiency is causing your gums to bleed, supplements may be recommended.

  • Surgical Interventions: In advanced cases, surgery may be required to restore gum health.

Regular dental visits and proper care are key to preventing and managing bleeding gums.
