Overview


Chipped Teeth


Having a chipped tooth means a fragment of your tooth enamel has broken off. Tooth enamel is the tough outer layer of your teeth that protects the sensitive tissue inside. Tooth enamel is sturdy, but it’s not indestructible


Symptoms and Causes


What are the symptoms of a chipped tooth?


Often, the only sign of a chipped tooth is a missing tooth fragment...


What causes a chipped tooth?


  • Injuries: Blows to your face or jaw, including falls, accidents, and sports injuries...

  • Chewing or biting on hard foods or objects: Biting down on hard foods, like an apple or hard candy...

  • Using your teeth to tear open packages: Most of us have used our teeth at some point to open stubborn packaging...

Risk Factors


Anything that puts you at risk of injury to your mouth (like playing a contact sport) increases the chances...


  • Eating lots of sugary or acidic foods: These foods wear down enamel over time...

  • GERD (gastroesophageal reflux disease): Acid reflux causes stomach acid to backwash into your mouth...

  • Previous dental work: Major dental work preserves your teeth but doesn’t make them as strong as they were before...

  • History of grinding your teeth: Grinding your teeth for a long time can thin the tooth structure...

  • Age: Teeth weaken over time. You’re more likely to chip your teeth if you’re over age 50...

Prevention


  • Wear a mouthguard during sports activities to protect your teeth from injury...

  • Avoid grinding your teeth...

  • Avoid using your teeth to open packages or bite on hard objects...

  • Maintain good dental hygiene and visit your dentist regularly...

  • Use fluoride toothpaste and mouthwash to strengthen your tooth enamel...

Diagnosis and Treatment


How is a chipped tooth treated?


Treatment options range from no treatment (for small chips) to major dental work to prevent further damage...


  • Polishing: For small chips, your provider may be able to polish and smooth the chip...

  • Bonding: Bonding uses a composite resin material to fill in the chip...

  • Veneers: Veneers are coverings that can hide imperfections in your front teeth...

  • Crowns: Crowns are tooth-shaped caps that protect a tooth that’s weak or has experienced major damage...

  • Fillings: Fillings use materials like metals, plastic, or glass to fill in missing parts...

  • Root canal: Root canals treat infections in the inner tissue of your tooth...

Outlook / Prognosis


Small chips in your teeth don’t usually cause lasting problems. Larger chips that expose the sensitive inner tissue...


Contact


If you think you have a chipped tooth, it’s best to see a dental provider as soon as possible...
