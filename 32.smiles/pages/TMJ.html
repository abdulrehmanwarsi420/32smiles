Temporomand ibular Joint (TMJ) Disorders


TMJ dysfunction (TMD) causes pain and tenderness in your jaw joints and surrounding muscles and ligaments. Causes include teeth grinding, jaw injuries, arthritis, and everyday wear and tear. TMJ treatment varies from person to person and may include medication, physical therapy, custom mouth guards, and jaw surgery.


Overview


What is TMJ dysfunction?


TMJ dysfunctions affect your jaw joints and surrounding muscles and ligaments, causing jaw pain, headaches, and difficulty opening and closing your mouth. TMJ refers to the joint, while TMD stands for temporomandibular joint dysfunction.

Types of TMD


  1. Disorders of your jaw joints.

  2. Disorders of your chewing muscles.

  3. Headaches that result from TMD.


Symptoms and Causes


What are the symptoms of TMJ dysfunction?


  • Jaw pain.

  • Facial pain.

  • Shoulder or neck pain.

  • Stiffness in your jaw.

  • Difficulty opening or closing your mouth.

  • Jaw popping or clicking.

  • Headaches or migraines.

  • Earaches or tinnitus.

  • Toothache or malocclusion.

Things that make TMJ dysfunction worse


  • Using your teeth as tools.

  • Poor posture.

  • Chewing on pens, pencils, or ice.

  • Excessive gum chewing.

  • Taking large bites of food.

  • Daytime teeth clenching or grinding.

  • Sleeping on your stomach.

Management and Treatment


How do healthcare providers treat TMJ dysfunction?


Treatment depends on the cause and severity of pain. Noninvasive options are tried first, such as medications, mouth guards, and physical therapy. Surgical options include arthrocentesis, TMJ arthroscopy, and open-joint surgery.


Medication


  • Pain relievers like acetaminophen.

  • NSAIDs like ibuprofen and naproxen.

  • Muscle relaxers for clenching or grinding.

  • Antidepressants to change pain interpretation.


Nonsurgical TMJ treatments


  • Mouth guards.

  • Physical therapy.

  • Trigger point injections.

  • Ultrasound therapy.

  • Transcutaneous electrical nerve stimulation (TENS).

  • Behavioral changes.


Surgical TMJ treatments


  • Arthrocentesis.

  • TMJ arthroscopy.

  • Open-joint surgery.

Prevention


Can I prevent TMJ dysfunction?


You can reduce your risk of TMD by wearing a mouth guard at night or during contact sports, practicing good posture, avoiding excessive gum chewing, and managing stress.
