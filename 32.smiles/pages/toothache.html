Toothache


A toothache can occur for many reasons, from a popcorn hull stuck in your gums to a bacterial infection. Temporary gum irritation can cause some toothaches, but serious ones need professional treatment.


Overview


What is a toothache?


A toothache refers to pain in or around your tooth. Minor toothaches from temporary gum irritation can be treated at home. Severe toothaches due to cavities, infection, or other conditions need professional dental treatment.


Severe tooth pain with fever and chills indicates a dental emergency. Contact a dentist or visit the nearest emergency room immediately.

Symptoms and Causes


What causes a toothache?


  • Cavities.

  • Abscessed tooth.

  • Cracked tooth.

  • A damaged dental restoration.

  • Teeth grinding or clenching.

  • Gum disease.

How long does a toothache last?


The duration of a toothache varies based on the cause. Temporary gum irritation may resolve in a day or two, but persistent pain may indicate a need for dental treatment.


Management and Treatment


How do dentists treat toothaches?


A dentist will examine your tooth, take X-rays if needed, and recommend treatment based on the severity of the issue.


Are there home remedies for toothaches?


Home remedies can provide temporary relief, but persistent tooth pain requires professional dental care.


Saltwater rinse


Mix 1/2 teaspoon of salt in 8 ounces of warm water, rinse for 30 seconds, then spit it out.


Hydrogen peroxide rinse


Dilute hydrogen peroxide with equal parts water, rinse, and spit it out.


Can I prevent toothaches?


Reduce your risk of toothaches by following good oral hygiene practices:


  • Brush your teeth twice a day.

  • Floss daily.

  • Visit your dentist regularly.

  • Avoid sugary foods and drinks.

  • Use a mouthguard if you grind or clench your teeth.
