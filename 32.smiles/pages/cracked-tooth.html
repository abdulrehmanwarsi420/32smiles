Cracked Tooth (Fractured Tooth)

A cracked or fractured tooth may happen because you grind your teeth, injure a tooth, or due to aging. A cracked tooth may cause symptoms like pain and swelling. Your dentist has several ways to treat a cracked tooth, depending on the crack’s location and severity.

Overview

What is a cracked tooth?

A cracked tooth is when something damages your tooth and causes a small break (crack or fracture). A badly cracked tooth may be a dental emergency.

Cracks happen most often in your upper front teeth and the back teeth of your lower jaw. You can have a crack in all parts of your teeth.

Types of cracked teeth

Your dentist may classify your tooth fracture as one of these five categories:

  • Cracked tooth: A vertical crack from the biting surface to your gum line.
  • Craze lines (hairline cracks): Small, thin cracks on your tooth enamel that don’t hurt.
  • Fractured cusp: A crack around a dental filling, usually not painful.
  • Split tooth: A crack splitting your tooth into two parts.
  • Vertical root fracture: A crack starting below your gum line moving toward the surface.

Is a cracked tooth a serious injury?

A crack in your tooth opens the door for bacteria to seep into your tooth pulp, causing a tooth abscess. Left untreated, this may lead to serious health issues.

Symptoms and Causes

What are the symptoms of a cracked tooth?

Symptoms include:

  • Sharp pain when biting down.
  • Sensitivity to temperature changes or sweet foods.
  • Swelling around the tooth.
  • Toothache when chewing.

Without treatment, a cracked tooth may lead to an infection (tooth abscess), with symptoms like bad breath, persistent tooth pain, fever, swollen gums, and lymph nodes.

What causes cracked teeth?

  • Age: Teeth wear down as you age, increasing the chance of cracks.
  • Biting hard foods: Ice, candy, and popcorn kernels can crack teeth.
  • Dental treatments: Large fillings or root canals can weaken teeth.
  • Bruxism: Teeth grinding from stress can lead to cracks.
  • Dental trauma: Accidents, sports injuries, or falls can cause cracks.

Diagnosis and Treatment

How is a cracked tooth diagnosed?

Your dentist may perform tests such as X-rays or tooth testing to diagnose a cracked tooth.

How is a cracked tooth treated?

  • Bonding: Applying a tooth-colored resin to cover a small crack.
  • Crown: A cap placed over a tooth to protect and restore its shape.
  • Root canal: Removing the tooth’s pulp if the crack has reached it.
  • Extraction: Removing a severely cracked or damaged tooth.

Early diagnosis and treatment are crucial to saving a cracked tooth. If you suspect a cracked tooth, see your dentist as soon as possible.
